Learning AI & LLMs

I spend a significant amount of my free time learning large language models the way I learn everything: by building real things with them, shipping to real users, and studying what works. AI is moving fast, and I treat keeping up with it as a core skill, not a hobby.

What I'm Learning Now

  • How to make LLM outputs trustworthy: grounding responses in reliable sources, reducing hallucination, and knowing when a model should say "I don't know."
  • Evaluating AI quality systematically instead of by gut feel, so improvements are measurable and regressions get caught.
  • Agentic and multi-step AI workflows, and where they genuinely beat a single well-designed prompt.
  • The economics of AI products: cost, latency, and quality tradeoffs that decide whether a feature is viable at scale.

How I Learn

  • Use AI to build AI: modern AI-assisted development tools are part of my daily workflow, which compounds how fast I can learn and iterate.
